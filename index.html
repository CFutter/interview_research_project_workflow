🛡 Security baseline — applies at every tier

These never relax, regardless of tier. Higher tiers add stricter controls on top.

› Full-disk encryption on every device that touches the data (BitLocker / FileVault).
› 3-2-1 backups: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 off-site — set up before the first interview.
› Store consent forms, the de-identification key, and the data in three separate places.
› A password manager for every project credential; strong, unique passwords.
› Never email unencrypted recordings (Tier 3+). Use Swiss-hosted, encrypted channels.
› Don't promise confidentiality you can't guarantee — the tier reduces risk, it can't erase it.
About this tool. A standalone, tier-adaptive companion to the SEC-HUM Sensitive Research Data Guide, developed for oral history at the University of Zurich and applicable across interview-based research. It reflects Swiss federal law (nFADP), Zurich cantonal law (IDG), and international oral history standards. It is practical guidance, not legal advice — for project-specific questions contact the UZH Delegate for Data Protection (datenschutz@dsd.uzh.ch) or your faculty's ethics committee. SEC-HUM is funded by swissuniversities (Open Science II / CHORD).
